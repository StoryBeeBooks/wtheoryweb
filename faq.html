Process & Timeline

What is your design process?

We follow the Design Thinking Framework, structured as a six-phase process:

  1. Phase 1: Empathize & Discovery – Stakeholder interviews, user research, competitive analysis
  2. Phase 2: Definition & Strategy – Information architecture, user journey mapping, wireframing
  3. Phase 3: Ideation & UI Design – Art direction, high-fidelity design, interactive prototyping
  4. Phase 4: Testing & Validation – Usability testing, accessibility audit, feedback integration
  5. Phase 5: Development Handoff – Design system creation, asset export, developer collaboration
  6. Phase 6: Launch & Iteration – QA testing, deployment, post-launch analytics, continuous optimization

This process is detailed on our Design Experience page.

Is accessibility important, and do you comply with WCAG and AODA?

Accessibility is a core priority for every project we undertake. We design and develop websites to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards and comply with Ontario's Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA). This includes proper semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility, sufficient color contrast, alternative text for images, and clear focus indicators. Accessible design is not only an ethical and legal requirement—it also improves SEO, expands your audience, and creates better user experiences for everyone. Learn more on our Accessibility Statement.
